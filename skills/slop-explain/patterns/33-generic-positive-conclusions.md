# Pattern 33: Generic positive conclusions

**Closing paragraphs that reach for warm, generic optimism: "The future looks bright," "Exciting times lie ahead."**

## Why LLMs do this

Training data is full of articles and essays that end on an upbeat note, especially in business writing, opinion columns, and motivational content. The model learned that "ending strong" means ending positively, and it deploys generic optimism as the default closing move.

The model also has no specific information about what should come next in the actual situation it is writing about. Lacking specifics, it reaches for the unspecific positive: bright futures, exciting times, promising horizons. The vagueness is what makes the closer usable in any context, which is also what makes it meaningless.

## Why it reads as AI

The closers are interchangeable. Any AI essay about any topic can end with "the future looks bright" and the sentence will technically work. That property (fitting anywhere) is exactly what makes the closer empty. A sentence that could end any article ends no article well.

The phrasing is also recognizable. "The future looks bright," "Exciting times lie ahead," "The possibilities are endless," "The journey has just begun." Each is a stock ending. None is doing work.

Real conclusions either summarize the argument, name specific next steps, or end on a specific image. AI conclusions float away on a warm cloud.

## Examples

After:
> The library expects to triple its membership by 2027 if the funding renewal passes.

Before:
> The future looks bright for the library, and exciting times lie ahead as it continues to serve the community.

After:
> I am going to keep using this framework. The bugs are real but the productivity wins are larger.

Before:
> All in all, the framework represents an exciting development, and the journey is just beginning.

After:
> What happens next depends on whether the FCC approves the rule change in November.

Before:
> The possibilities are endless, and the future of the industry looks bright.

Notice that the human versions end on something specific and consequential. The AI versions end on a generic feeling.

## How to self-spot

Read the last paragraph of your draft. Does it contain phrases like "the future looks bright," "exciting times," "the possibilities are endless," "the journey is just beginning," "promising horizons"? If yes, those phrases are doing no work.

Replace the closer with one of:
- A specific prediction with a date or condition
- A specific next step (yours or the subject's)
- A specific image or moment that sticks
- Nothing. Sometimes the strongest ending is just the second-to-last paragraph.

A useful test: cover the last paragraph and read what comes before. If the piece ends well without the closer, the closer was filler.
